<p><p>High power attenuators are used to reduce the power of a signal without significantly distorting its waveform. There are three main types: chip-based, waveguide, and coaxial. Chip-based attenuators use semiconductor technology and are suitable for a wide range of applications. Waveguide attenuators are used in high-frequency and high-power systems where low insertion loss and high power handling capabilities are required. Coaxial attenuators are designed for use in coaxial transmission systems, offering a compact and versatile solution for power reduction while maintaining signal integrity.</p></p>
