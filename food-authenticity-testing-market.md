<p><p>Food authenticity testing is conducted to ensure the accuracy of food labeling claims and to detect any adulteration or fraud in the food supply chain. Several methods are employed in this testing, including polymerase chain reaction (PCR), liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (LC-MS), isotope methods, immunoassays, and others. PCR is used to identify genetic material and determine the presence of specific ingredients or species. LC-MS detects and quantifies chemical compounds, while isotope methods analyze the stable isotopes in food to trace their origin. Immunoassays detect and quantify proteins and other molecules. Other methods include spectroscopy, chromatography, and electrophoresis.</p></p>
