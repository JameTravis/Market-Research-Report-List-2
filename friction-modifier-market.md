<p><p>Friction modifiers are additives used in lubricants to reduce friction and enhance performance. There are two main types of friction modifiers: organic and inorganic. Organic friction modifiers are derived from organic compounds such as fatty acids and esters. They provide excellent lubrication and reduce wear and tear. In contrast, inorganic friction modifiers are usually solid particles such as molybdenum disulfide or graphite. They create a protective layer on metal surfaces, reducing friction and improving efficiency. Both types of friction modifiers have their own advantages and applications in various industries.</p></p>
