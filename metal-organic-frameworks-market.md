<p><p>Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs) are a class of materials built by linking metal ions or clusters with organic ligands through coordination bonds. These materials possess highly ordered structures with regular porosity, making them ideal for applications in various industries. MOFs have a wide range of potential uses, including gas storage and separation, catalysis, drug delivery, sensors, and electronics.</p><p>The future outlook for the metal-organic frameworks market appears promising. The market is expected to witness significant growth due to the increasing demand for MOFs in gas storage and separation applications. MOFs offer high surface areas, large pore volumes, and tunable chemical functionalities, making them suitable for storing gases like hydrogen and carbon dioxide. With the growing focus on renewable energy sources and the need to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, the demand for MOFs for gas storage and separation is expected to rise.</p><p>Moreover, MOFs also hold great potential in the field of catalysis. Their unique chemical properties allow for the design of efficient catalysts for various chemical reactions. This has led to increasing research and development activities in the field, driving the demand for MOFs.</p><p>In addition, the healthcare industry presents another promising avenue for the application of MOFs.
