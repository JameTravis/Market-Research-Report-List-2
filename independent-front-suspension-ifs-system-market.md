<p><p>The Independent Front Suspension (IFS) system is a type of automotive suspension that allows each front wheel to move independently of the other. It offers various market types such as the Double-transverse Wishbone Suspension, MacPherson Suspension, and Short Swinging Arm. </p><p>Double-transverse Wishbone Suspension is characterized by two wishbone-shaped control arms attached to the chassis, allowing for excellent handling and stability. </p><p>MacPherson Suspension features a single control arm attached to the wheel hub, providing simplicity, cost-effectiveness, and space-saving benefits. </p><p>Short Swinging Arm incorporates two short, relocating arms for each wheel, ensuring enhanced ride quality and stability. These market types provide different options for automakers to choose from based on their specific design and performance requirements.</p></p>
