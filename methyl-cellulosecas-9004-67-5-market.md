<p><p>Methyl Cellulose (CAS 9004-67-5) is a chemically modified cellulose derivative that is commonly used as a thickening and stabilizing agent in various industries. It is made by treating cellulose with a strong alkali solution and then reacting it with methyl chloride. This process results in the substitution of hydroxyl groups with methyl groups, making the cellulose more water-soluble and resistant to enzymatic degradation.</p><p>Currently, the Methyl Cellulose market is witnessing steady growth due to its increasing usage in the construction, pharmaceutical, and food industries. In the construction industry, Methyl Cellulose is commonly used as a thickener in cement and mortar, providing enhanced workability and improved adhesion properties. In the pharmaceutical industry, it is used as a binder, disintegrant, and film-forming agent in tablet formulations. In the food industry, it is widely utilized as a stabilizer and thickener in various food applications, such as sauces, dressings, and dairy products.</p><p>The future outlook of the Methyl Cellulose market seems optimistic, with a projected CAGR of 13.3% during the forecasted period. Furthermore, the rising trend of clean-label and natural ingredients in the food industry is likely to fuel the demand for Methyl Cellulose as a food additive.</p><p>Overall, the Methyl Cellulose market is poised for significant growth in the coming years, driven by its versatile applications and increasing demand across various industries.</p></p>
